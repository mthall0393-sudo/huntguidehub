Turkey Hunting Season Checklist: Everything You Need

Spring turkey season is one of the most exhilarating times for upland hunters. To make the most of it, you need a solid plan and the right gear. Use this checklist to stay organized and focused.

Core Checklist Items

  • License & Permit: Obtain a valid state hunting license and any required turkey stamps.
  • Firearm or Bow: A 12‑gauge shotgun (2¾" or 3" chambers) or a bow with broadhead arrows. Check U.S. Fish & Wildlife Service regulations for legal draw weights.
  • Camouflage & Concealment: Camo clothing, face paint, and a portable blind or natural cover.
  • Calls & Decoys: Turkey calls (box, slate, mouth) and a simple decoy spread (2‑4 birds) to attract gobblers.
  • Safety Gear: Orange safety vests, ear protection, and a first‑aid kit.

Additional Gear for Comfort

  • Sturdy hiking boots with ankle support
  • Weather‑appropriate layers (wicking base, insulated mid‑layer, waterproof outer)
  • Water‑proof field bag for snacks and extra ammunition
  • Binoculars for scouting early morning roosts

Pre‑Season Prep

  1. Scout local turkey roosts using a map or a field app (e.g., OnX Hunt).
  2. Practice calling at home or at a range to develop realistic gobble sounds.
  3. Set up your blind a day before the hunt to avoid spooking birds.
  4. Check wind direction – turkeys have keen sense of smell.
