Antelope Hunting: How to Draw Tags and Find Guides

Pronghorn antelope offer one of the fastest‑moving experiences in North American big‑game hunting. Success hinges on securing a tag early and partnering with a knowledgeable guide who knows the terrain, migration patterns, and safety protocols.

1. Understand the Tag‑Drawing Process

  • State‑Specific Draws: Most antelope tags are allocated through a lottery. Check the Wyoming Game & Fish Department, Colorado Division of Wildlife, and Idaho Department of Fish & Game for draw dates and application windows.
  • Eligibility: You typically need a valid hunting license, a recent hunting record (or a “first‑time hunter” waiver), and to be at least 18 years old.
  • Application Tips:
    1. Register online as soon as the application portal opens (usually early March).
    2. Provide accurate personal information; typos can disqualify your entry.
    3. Enter multiple draw zones if allowed – increasing your odds of a win.

2. Choosing the Right Antelope Guide

A good guide can turn a drawn tag into a trophy. Look for these key factors:

  1. Licensing & Permits: Verify the guide holds a current state hunting guide license (e.g., Wyoming Guide License).
  2. Success Statistics: Reputable outfitters publish harvest numbers or success rates for antelope. Guides with transparent stats (e.g., "85% pronghorn success 2025") are typically more reliable.
  3. Specialization: Some outfits focus exclusively on antelope (e.g., Rocky Creek Outfitters), while others offer mixed big‑game packages. Pick one that lists antelope as a primary species.
  4. Safety & Support: Pre‑hunt safety briefings, first‑aid kits, and reliable communication plans are non‑negotiable.
  5. Equipment Rental: If you don’t own a 20‑22 ga rifle, thermal optics, or spotting scopes, confirm the guide provides rentals.

3. Gear Checklist for Antelope Hunts

  • Rifle: 20‑22 ga with a 28‑30 in barrel for flat trajectory.
  • Optics: 8‑12× spotting scope or high‑magnification binoculars.
  • Thermal / Night Vision: Helpful for low‑light drives.
  • Clothing: Layered, camouflaged, with wind‑proof outerwear.
  • Backpack: Water, high‑energy snacks, range‑finder, and a lightweight field‑rubbing kit.

4. Hunt Planning Tips

  1. Scout the area using satellite imagery (Google Earth) and public land maps.
  2. Identify water sources, travel corridors, and wind‑driven ridgelines where antelope funnel.
  3. Schedule early‑morning drives; pronghorn are most active at dawn.
  4. Practice long‑range shooting (300‑500 yd) before the season.
