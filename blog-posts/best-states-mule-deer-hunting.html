Best States for Mule Deer Hunting You Need to Try

Mule deer are known for their stunning antlers and wide‑ranging habits, making them a prized target for many hunters. Below are the top states where you’ll find the healthiest populations, diverse terrain, and reliable hunting opportunities.

Why These States Stand Out

  • Habitat Variety: From high desert mesas to lush mountain valleys, each state offers distinct environments that challenge different hunting styles.
  • Season Length & Bag Limits: Many states have extended seasons or flexible bag limits, giving hunters more chances to succeed.
  • Public Access: Robust public land systems (National Forests, BLM, state wildlife areas) make it easier for independent hunters to get on the ground.

Top Mule Deer Hunting States

  1. Colorado – Home to the Rocky Mountains, Colorado features high‑altitude stands and spectacular scenery. Public lands like the Pike‑National Forest provide abundant mule deer, and guides such as Cherokee T.P. Outfitters or Gunnison Country Outfitters specialize in mule deer as well as elk.
  2. Idaho – Known for its rugged backcountry, Idaho’s Salmon and Sawtooth ranges host prolific mule deer herds. The Idaho Hunting Guide Association lists licensed outfitters; notable names include Idaho Deer Tours.
  3. Montana – With vast public acreage in the western third of the state, Montana offers high‑quality mule deer. Outfitters like Elk Ridge Outfitters and Montana Big Game Outfitters often run dedicated mule deer trips.
  4. Wyoming – The high plains and mountain valleys of Wyoming sustain healthy mule deer populations. Guides such as Table Mountain Outfitters provide tailored mule deer hunts.
  5. Utah – The desert‑mountain mix of Utah yields big‑antlered mule deer, especially in the Wasatch‑Cache National Forest. Look for Utah Deer Outfitters for season‑specific packages.

Tips for a Successful Mule Deer Hunt

  • Scout Early: Use Google Earth or apps like OnX Hunt to locate water sources and travel corridors.
  • Early‑Morning Drives: Mule deer are most active at dawn; position yourself on known trails.
  • Use Spotting Scopes: A 20‑40x scope helps spot distant bucks on ridgelines.
  • Stay Mobile: Mule deer cover large distances; be prepared to move after a few minutes of inactivity.
